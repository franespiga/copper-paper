\section{Reproducibility and data availability statement}
\label{sec:reproducibility}

The analysis is designed to be reproducible from repository scripts under the MIT license.
All figures and tables intended for the paper are generated by the \copper{} pipeline and
synced into \texttt{paper/figures} and \texttt{paper/tables} via \texttt{make paper-assets}.
Data provenance is recorded in \texttt{data/metadata/} and summarized in supplementary reports.
Random seeds are configured in \texttt{configs/scenarios.yaml} and related configuration files.
Known limitations and missing datasets are logged in \texttt{paper/MANUSCRIPT\_LIMITATIONS.md}.

Public release of underlying third-party datasets depends on source licenses (DEFRA, AHDB, and
reference tables documented in \texttt{data/metadata/sources.csv}).
We do \textbf{not} claim full reproducibility until \texttt{make reproduce} and
\texttt{make paper-pdf} succeed on a clean environment with the recorded package versions.

Code, configuration, experiment registry entries, and run metadata are available in the main
\copper{} repository; the Overleaf-oriented submission package excludes raw and processed data
by design.
