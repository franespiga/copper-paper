\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:introduction}

Seasonal crop choice exposes UK farmers to correlated agronomic and market risk.
Individual diversification within a county may be limited by land, rotation, and local
agronomic constraints, while spatial diversification across counties is difficult to
coordinate without institutional arrangements.

This paper asks whether \emph{cooperative seasonal crop allocation}---with explicit
risk-sharing rules and spatial diversification---can improve member welfare relative to
decentralized benchmarks.
Our unit of analysis is county $\times$ crop $\times$ season, and the empirical setting
is UK arable agriculture for a core crop universe selected by a documented Pareto procedure.

\coppercontrib{}
The contribution is cooperative seasonal allocation, spatial diversification, and
member-level welfare evaluation under uncertainty---not price forecasting, machine learning,
structural supply--demand estimation, or national central planning.
