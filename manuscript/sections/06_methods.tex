\section{Methods}
\label{sec:methods}

\subsection{Scenario generation}

We evaluate strategies under:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item historical backtests on observed seasons;
  \item dependence-preserving Monte Carlo bootstrap resampling of the profit panel; and
  \item price-scenario robustness (observed, rolling average, adverse shock, oversupply penalties).
\end{enumerate}
Oversupply penalties are robustness scenarios, not identified structural price effects.

\subsection{Portfolio optimization}

Mean--variance and CVaR objectives are solved with documented constraints (long-only weights,
maximum crop share caps, feasible crop--region sets).
Covariance matrices are estimated with sample, shrinkage, and robust estimators; sensitivity
is reported in robustness tables.

\subsection{Inference}

Hypothesis tests compare cooperative and independent benchmarks with paired tests,
bootstrap confidence intervals, and effect sizes where configured.
Random seeds and package versions are fixed in configuration files.

\subsection{Forecasting (auxiliary)}

Walk-forward price forecast evaluation (naive, rolling mean, seasonal naive, gradient boosting)
supports appendix discussion only and does not enter main cooperative allocation results unless
forecasts beat naive baselines.
