\section{Related literature and research gap}
\label{sec:literature}

Agricultural portfolio and risk-programming models study how crop mix affects expected
returns and downside risk \citep{hardaker2015,Bradford1997}.
Spatial diversification and cooperative risk pooling appear in regional risk-management
and insurance literatures, but cooperative \emph{seasonal} allocation with explicit
sharing rules and member-level welfare metrics remains less developed for UK county-scale
crop portfolios.

Our gap is institutional: we evaluate cooperative coordination across counties and crops
under uncertainty, with benchmarks that separate independent behavior, local cooperation,
regional coordination, and a national upper-bound benchmark that is not offered as policy advice.

Price forecasting and machine learning may inform scenario design but are auxiliary in this
paper; main results rely on observed and resampled historical profit panels unless naive
forecasts demonstrably improve scenario generation (see appendix forecast metrics).
