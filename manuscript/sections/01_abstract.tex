\begin{abstract}
UK arable farmers face correlated yield, price, and cost shocks across counties and crops.
We study whether seasonal cooperative crop allocation can reduce income volatility and
member welfare dispersion relative to decentralized benchmarks.
Using a county--crop--season profit panel and a reproducible \copper{} pipeline,

We compare observed allocations, independent profit-maximizing and diversified farmers,
and local, regional, and national cooperative benchmarks under historical backtests,
bootstrap scenarios, and price-scenario robustness checks.
Results are reported with paired hypothesis tests and bootstrap uncertainty where available.
